MYDI  ·  Peer-Led Health Navigation

Restoring life-saving ART and PrEP access for high-risk youth in Masaka through trusted, discreet, community-led health services.

⚠ Urgent — Treatment Interruption Crisis

A funding withdrawal at TASO Masaka Hospital DIC has left hundreds of high-risk youth without a safe pathway to treatment. MYDI is ready to bridge this gap — immediately.

Who We Are

Built by the people who ran the system.

MYDI was founded by the same social workers and community coordinators who delivered HIV prevention and treatment services at the TASO Masaka Hospital Drop-In Centre. When funding was withdrawn, we did not walk away.

We have the patient data, the clinical relationships, the community trust, and the peer-led infrastructure already in place. We are not starting from scratch — we are asking for the resources to continue what was already working.

The Crisis

Two red flags. Hundreds of lives at risk.

Treatment Interruption: High-risk youth who were receiving ART and PrEP through the DIC have lost their safe access point. Without continuity, treatment interruption leads directly to increased transmission and mortality.

System Collapse: The trusted infrastructure — peer navigators, outreach workers, data systems — built over years is at risk of permanent dissolution. Once this network dissolves, it cannot be easily rebuilt.

MYDI exists to prevent both from happening.
